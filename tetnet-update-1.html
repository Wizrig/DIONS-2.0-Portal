Legacy DIONS to DIONS 2.0: Full Side-by-Side Technical Update

Legacy DIONS under IOCoin already had real identity, messaging, data, encryption, privacy, and PoS operation. DIONS 2.0 is a deep architecture expansion and hardening program, not a cosmetic feature refresh. This update shows exactly what changed, what was tested, what failed and was corrected, and what still must be proven before release.

RC Candidate: 03bd440 Automated Suites: 661 / 661 PASS Subsystem Sweep: 23 / 23 Functional PASS Conservative Readiness: 65–72%

What legacy had, and why DIONS 2.0 exists

Legacy IOCoin DIONS already provided alias identity and alias-linked data, public/encrypted messaging APIs, file/data upload-download-transfer flows, AES-256 + RSA-4096 cryptographic messaging/data handling, shade privacy payment API, and live PoS chain behavior.

DIONS 2.0 was started to make those capabilities deterministic under contention, scalable under load, extensible for modern execution (EVM/SVM), stronger on network resilience, and ready for hybrid post-quantum hardening.

What we fixed and what still needs release gates

Fixed classes: deterministic sender/nonce/auth mismatch, mempool reject-loop contamination, large payload relay divergence, and hybrid/PQC consistency defects from earlier RC runs.

Still required before release: 24h/72h no-intervention soaks, sustained mixed throughput with p50/p95/p99 metrics, fault campaigns (churn/loss/latency/partitions), crash/restart/resync determinism, and bridge operational rollback drills.
