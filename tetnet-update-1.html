DIONS 2.0 Legacy-to-RC Technical Status

Legacy DIONS under IOCoin was already a working identity/data/messaging system, not a zero-start prototype. It delivered alias identity and alias-linked data, encrypted and public messaging APIs, file transfer paths, AES-256 + RSA-4096 cryptographic flows, shade privacy payment support, and active PoS chain operation.

DIONS 2.0 has been a deep protocol hardening and expansion effort: deterministic consensus behavior under contention, stronger relay behavior, modern execution surfaces (EVM and SVM/dispute), bridge/interop mechanics, agent framework, and hybrid post-quantum security paths. Over the recent cycle, testing moved from root-cause triage to replay validation, multi-node feature gates, and subsystem-wide RC validation windows.

Legacy baseline and DIONS 2.0 scope

The move to DIONS 2.0 is a fundamental architecture shift. Legacy functionality was preserved, then hardened and extended for deterministic execution, higher reliability, broader smart execution capability, and stricter validation behavior under live multi-node conditions.

Failure classes corrected in this cycle

  • Fixed: deterministic sender/nonce/auth mismatch across wallet, mempool, and validation.
  • Fixed: metered transaction reject-loop contamination affecting liveness.
  • Fixed: relay divergence from non-blocking large payload transport behavior.
  • Fixed: hybrid/PQC consistency defects seen in earlier RC windows.
